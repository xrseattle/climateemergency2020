Declare Climate Emergency in Your State or Local Government

Indigenous People Demand Declaration of Climate Emergency

It’s 2020. We’ve entered the last decade in which to take effective action to avert the worst possible climate catastrophes. Indigenous leaders are holding state and local governments accountable to tell the truth: we are in a climate emergency. “Our children, their children, all children, deserve a future free of the threat of climate disaster and toxic water,” says Saanich activist Paul Chiyokten Wagner, “It’s their birthright. All of the creations of the Creator deserve a livable planet and it is our human responsibility to protect the circle of life while there is still time."

The time for incremental change has come and gone. Our work now is to force climate emergency to the center of political action. In Washington State, Protectors of the Salish Sea, an action organization founded by Chiyokten, is leading the campaign to enact state government emergency measures since their 46-mile prayer walk and occupation of the state capitol grounds in 2019.

While over a thousand local governments worldwide have declared a climate emergency or enacted resolutions for a Green New Deal, there are many more that have not, and a single state government has yet to do so.

It is for these reasons, and many others, that we call on indigenous protectors of the sacred and all of our allies, wherever you are, to bring the demand for a declaration of climate emergency to the seat of your state or local government with bold, direct actions. A government that declares climate emergency is facing the truth and putting the well-being of its people first.

The minimum emergency actions are

  • Place a moratorium on permits for new fossil fuel projects
  • Impanel a citizens assembly to define policies that support the needs of the residents of the locality or state

About

Paul Chiyokten Wagner

Paul Chiyokten Wagner, Saanich tribal member, is the founder of Protectors of the Salish Sea and the Tarpee housing project at Standing Rock.

Protectors of the Salish Sea

The mission of Protectors of the Salish Sea is to end the era of fossil fuel in our Salish Sea and beyond. We work to bring long-lasting harmony and true peace among all beings back to our Salish Sea and beyond through the recognition and understandings of who we all are as human beings: a return to who The Creator intended us to be–Protectors who take care of all life. The very water that flows through our veins is the same ancestral water we are protecting for our future generations. Please support the work in Washington State by signing our petition.
